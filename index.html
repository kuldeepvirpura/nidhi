Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death globally. An estimated 17.9 million people died from CVDs in 2019, representing 32% of all global deaths. Of these deaths, 85% were due to heart attack and stroke. Over three quarters of CVD deaths take place in low- and middle-income countries. In 2016, the estimated prevalence of CVDs in India was estimated to be 54.5 million. One in 4 deaths in India are now because of CVDs with ischemic heart disease and stroke responsible for >80% of this burden. The WHO prescribes a doctor population ratio of 1:1000. The doctor-population ratio of the some of the countries are: India - 1:1000, Australia - 3.374:1000, Brazil - 1.852:1000, China - 1.49:1000, France - 3.227:1000, Germany - 4.125:1000, Russia - 3.306:1000, the USA - 2.554:1000, Afghanistan - 0.304:1000, Bangladesh - 0.389:1000, Pakistan - 0.806:1000. At present, it is estimated that the number of cardiologists in India is 1 for every 300,000 persons. Despite losing 1.7 million lives to heart disease every year, India does not have enough cardiologists to manage the burden. India needs 88,000 cardiologists. Due to less number of cardiologists, they demand more money which is one of the factory why heart surgeries are expensive. The cost of surgery also depends on many factors, namely: Type of surgery and consumables, equipment required. Expertise of the cardiothoracic surgeon and team. Underlying medical condition of the patient, which can affect the hospital stay, additional tests and medicine requirement.
